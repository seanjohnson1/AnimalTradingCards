African Penguin

The African penguin, also known as the jackass penguin and black-footed penguin is a species of penguin, confined to southern African waters

  • Scientific Name: Spheniscus demersus
  • Common Name: African Penguin
  • Other Name(s): Jackass Penguin
  • Average Lifespan: 10 - 15 years
  • Habitat: Rocky Ocean Islands

African penguins grow to 60–70 cm (24–28 in) tall and weigh between 2.2–3.5 kg (4.9–7.7 lb).[6] They have a black stripe and black spots on the chest, the pattern of spots being unique for every penguin, like human fingerprints. They have pink glands above their eyes, which are used for thermoregulation. The hotter the penguin gets, the more blood is sent to these glands so it may be cooled by the surrounding air, thus making the glands more pink.
